# Weather Guidelines for Rowers

Understanding and respecting weather conditions is essential for safe rowing. This guide will help you recognize when it's safe to row and when to stay on land.

<div class="info-box warning mb-3">
  <h4>Safety First</h4>
  <p>Your coach will make the final decision on whether conditions are safe for rowing. However, all rowers should understand basic weather safety guidelines and speak up if they have concerns.</p>
</div>

## Weather Conditions to Watch

### When NOT to Row

<div class="two-col-grid">
  <div>
    <h4>Lightning/Thunder</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>If you see lightning or hear thunder, get off the water immediately</li>
      <li>Remain off the water for at least 30 minutes after the last lightning flash or thunder sound</li>
      <li>Do not count on having warning time – lightning can strike without notice</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4>Cold Conditions</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Do not row when air temperature + water temperature is below 100°F (37.8°C)</li>
      <li>Water below 50°F (10°C) is extremely dangerous</li>
      <li>Cold water immersion can cause incapacitation within minutes</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="two-col-grid">
  <div>
    <h4>Wind/Waves</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Avoid rowing when wind exceeds 15 mph (24 km/h)</li>
      <li>Stay off the water when whitecaps are widespread</li>
      <li>Novices should be more conservative with wind limits</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4>Visibility</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Do not row if you cannot see at least 100 meters (328 ft) in any direction</li>
      <li>Fog, heavy rain, and darkness can all create hazardous visibility conditions</li>
      <li>If you cannot clearly see shoreline reference points, do not row</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="info-box tip mb-3">
  <h4>Quick Check Guide</h4>
  <p>When in doubt, use this simple rule: if conditions seem questionable, they probably are. Missing one practice is better than risking your safety.</p>
</div>

## Weather Indicators to Understand

### Wind Effects

| Wind Speed | Water Appearance | What It Means for Rowers |
|------------|------------------|--------------------------|
| 0-7 mph | Smooth to small ripples | Safe for all rowers |
| 8-12 mph | Small waves, occasional whitecaps | Challenging for beginners, manageable for experienced |
| 13-18 mph | Moderate waves, frequent whitecaps | Difficult for all, not recommended for novices |
| Above 18 mph | Large waves, whitecaps, spray | Unsafe for regular shells |

<div class="info-box note mb-3">
  <h4>Wind Direction Matters</h4>
  <p>A tailwind on the way out means a headwind on the return. Always consider if you'll be able to return safely, especially if conditions are worsening.</p>
</div>

### Before You Head to Practice

1. **Check the forecast** for your entire planned rowing time
2. **Look for sudden changes** in temperature, wind, or precipitation
3. **Be aware of weather warnings** or advisories for your area
4. **Communicate with coaches** if you have questions about conditions
5. **Dress appropriately** for the conditions

<div class="info-box warning mb-3">
  <h4>Trust Your Instincts</h4>
  <p>If you're uncomfortable with weather conditions, don't row. Your coaches will respect your decision to prioritize safety.</p>
</div>

## Preparing for Different Weather Conditions

### Cold Weather Rowing

<div class="two-col-grid">
  <div>
    <h4>Proper Clothing</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Dress in layers that can be adjusted while rowing</li>
      <li>Use moisture-wicking base layers</li>
      <li>Protect extremities with appropriate gloves, hat, socks</li>
      <li>Consider a splash jacket in cold, windy conditions</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4>Safety Precautions</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Row in groups or with coach supervision</li>
      <li>Stay closer to shore than usual</li>
      <li>Know the symptoms of hypothermia</li>
      <li>Carry a change of clothes in a waterproof bag</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

### Hot Weather Rowing

<div class="two-col-grid">
  <div>
    <h4>Preparation</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Hydrate well before, during, and after rowing</li>
      <li>Use sunscreen and reapply as needed</li>
      <li>Consider lightweight, light-colored clothing</li>
      <li>Wear a hat and sunglasses for sun protection</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4>Warning Signs</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Headache or dizziness</li>
      <li>Excessive sweating or inability to sweat</li>
      <li>Nausea or vomiting</li>
      <li>Confusion or disorientation</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
