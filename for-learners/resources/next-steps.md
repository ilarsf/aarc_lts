# Next Steps After Learn to Scull

Congratulations on completing the Learn to Scull program! This guide outlines your options for continuing your rowing journey with the Ann Arbor Rowing Club. Whether you're ready for independent sculling or need more supervised practice, there's a pathway for you to continue developing your skills.

## Immediate Next Steps

<div class="row">
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h3>Complete Your Paperwork</h3>
      <p>Before continuing with rowing, make sure you:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Receive your skills assessment from coaches</li>
        <li>Complete club membership paperwork (if not already done)</li>
        <li>Sign safety acknowledgment forms</li>
        <li>Set up your account in the club's online system</li>
        <li>Review the full club safety rules and policies</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h3>Assess Your Readiness</h3>
      <p>Consider these questions honestly:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Do you feel confident in your safety knowledge?</li>
        <li>Can you consistently control the boat in normal conditions?</li>
        <li>Are you comfortable with the club's traffic patterns?</li>
        <li>Can you safely respond to other water traffic?</li>
        <li>Do you know when not to row based on conditions?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Pathways Based on Your Assessment Level

### Level 1: Developing Scullers

<div class="info-box">
  <p>If your assessment indicated you need more supervised practice:</p>
  <h4>Recommended Next Programs:</h4>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Extension Lessons:</strong> 2-4 additional supervised sessions focused on specific skills</li>
    <li><strong>Mentored Rowing:</strong> Paired sessions with experienced club members</li>
    <li><strong>Supervised Practice Sessions:</strong> Group rowing with coach in launch</li>
    <li><strong>Fall Learn to Scull 2:</strong> A follow-up program for graduates needing more practice</li>
  </ul>
  <h4>Practice Focus:</h4>
  <ul>
    <li>Boat balance and control</li>
    <li>Basic stroke sequencing</li>
    <li>Launching and landing</li>
    <li>Steering fundamentals</li>
  </ul>
  <p><strong>Typical Timeline:</strong> 1-2 months of additional supervised practice before independent sculling</p>
</div>

### Level 2: Competent Scullers

<div class="info-box">
  <p>If your assessment indicated you're ready for limited independent rowing:</p>
  <h4>Recommended Next Programs:</h4>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Novice Program:</strong> Structured group rowing with coaching</li>
    <li><strong>Limited Independent Sculling:</strong> Solo rowing in designated areas during daylight hours</li>
    <li><strong>Skills Development Clinics:</strong> Focused technical sessions on specific aspects</li>
    <li><strong>Novice Crew Formation:</strong> Introduction to team sculling in quads</li>
  </ul>
  <h4>Practice Focus:</h4>
  <ul>
    <li>Refining technique and power application</li>
    <li>Developing stamina and consistency</li>
    <li>Building navigation confidence</li>
    <li>Intermediate rowing skills</li>
  </ul>
  <p><strong>Typical Timeline:</strong> Ready for most club programs with some limitations</p>
</div>

### Level 3: Proficient Scullers

<div class="info-box">
  <p>If your assessment indicated you're ready for full independent sculling:</p>
  <h4>Recommended Next Programs:</h4>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Full Independent Sculling:</strong> Access to club singles during all rowing hours</li>
    <li><strong>Intermediate Technique Clinics:</strong> Advanced technical development</li>
    <li><strong>Club Rows:</strong> Group rowing events and social rows</li>
    <li><strong>Racing Development:</strong> Introduction to competitive opportunities</li>
  </ul>
  <h4>Practice Focus:</h4>
  <ul>
    <li>Building speed and efficiency</li>
    <li>Refining advanced technique</li>
    <li>Developing fitness and endurance</li>
    <li>Potentially preparing for competition</li>
  </ul>
  <p><strong>Typical Timeline:</strong> Immediate access to full club sculling programs</p>
</div>

## Ongoing Club Programs

### Structured Programs

<div class="row">
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h4>Novice Program (Seasonal)</h4>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Who it's for:</strong> Recent Learn to Scull graduates</li>
        <li><strong>Format:</strong> 2-3 coached sessions weekly</li>
        <li><strong>Focus:</strong> Technique refinement and conditioning</li>
        <li><strong>Equipment:</strong> Club singles and introduce quads</li>
        <li><strong>Schedule:</strong> Usually early mornings or evenings</li>
        <li><strong>Duration:</strong> 8-week seasons</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h4>Skills Development Clinics</h4>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Who it's for:</strong> Rowers of all levels</li>
        <li><strong>Format:</strong> Single-topic focused sessions</li>
        <li><strong>Focus:</strong> Specific technical elements</li>
        <li><strong>Equipment:</strong> Varies by clinic</li>
        <li><strong>Schedule:</strong> Weekends, usually 2-3 hours</li>
        <li><strong>Topics Include:</strong> Advanced balance, blade work, steering, racing starts, etc.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

### Independent Rowing Options

<div class="row">
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h4>Open Rowing</h4>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Who it's for:</strong> Level 2-3 scullers</li>
        <li><strong>Format:</strong> Self-directed practice</li>
        <li><strong>Equipment:</strong> Club singles (reservation required)</li>
        <li><strong>Schedule:</strong> During club hours (seasonal)</li>
        <li><strong>Requirements:</strong> Follow sign-out procedures</li>
        <li><strong>Safety:</strong> Row with buddy or monitor on duty</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h4>Club Rows</h4>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Who it's for:</strong> All members</li>
        <li><strong>Format:</strong> Group rows with mixed ability levels</li>
        <li><strong>Equipment:</strong> Singles and team boats</li>
        <li><strong>Schedule:</strong> Typically weekend mornings</li>
        <li><strong>Focus:</strong> Social rowing and club community</li>
        <li><strong>Distance:</strong> Usually 5-10km at conversational pace</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Development Recommendations by Interest

<div class="row">
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h3>For Recreational Scullers</h3>
      <p>If you want to enjoy rowing for fitness and pleasure:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Focus on technique refinement and efficiency</li>
        <li>Participate in club social rows</li>
        <li>Consider expanding to scenic rows when ready</li>
        <li>Try different boats as you progress (recreational doubles, etc.)</li>
        <li>Develop at your own pace without competitive pressure</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <div class="info-box">
      <h3>For Competitive Development</h3>
      <p>If you're interested in racing opportunities:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Join the novice racing program</li>
        <li>Incorporate structured training and conditioning</li>
        <li>Participate in technique clinics and video analysis</li>
        <li>Train for local regattas and head races</li>
        <li>Consider joining team boats for competition</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Long-Term Rowing Development

<div class="info-box info">
  <h4>Your First Year of Rowing</h4>
  <p>A typical development path in your first year might include:</p>
  <ol>
    <li><strong>Months 1-2:</strong> Learn to Scull + extension lessons or novice program</li>
    <li><strong>Months 3-4:</strong> Regular practice focusing on consistency and technique</li>
    <li><strong>Months 5-6:</strong> Introduction to team boats (doubles/quads) and longer distances</li>
    <li><strong>Months 7-9:</strong> Participate in club rows, develop endurance and skills</li>
    <li><strong>Months 10-12:</strong> Potential first low-key racing experiences or longer distance rows</li>
  </ol>
  <p>Remember that everyone progresses at their own pace - this timeline is just a general guideline!</p>
</div>

## Get Involved in the Club

<div class="info-box tip">
