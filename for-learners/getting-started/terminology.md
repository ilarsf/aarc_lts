    <div class="tab-content active" id="all"> <!-- Changed class and id -->
        <div class="tab-content-inner"> <!-- Added tab-content-inner div -->
            <h2 class="az-glossary-letter">All Essential Terms</h2>
            
            <dl class="glossary"> <!-- Using <dl> for terms and definitions -->
                <dt>Back/Back it Down</dt>
                <dd>Command to row in reverse.</dd>
                
                <dt>Blade/Spoon</dt>
                <dd>The wide, flat end of the oar that goes in the water.</dd>
                
                <dt>Bow</dt>
                <dd>The front end of the boat (crosses the finish line first).</dd>
                
                <dt>Catch</dt>
                <dd>The moment when the blade enters the water.</dd>
                
                <dt>Cockpit</dt>
                <dd>The area where the rower sits.</dd>
                
                <dt>Collar/Button</dt>
                <dd>The plastic piece that prevents the oar from sliding through the oarlock.</dd>
                
                <dt>Crab</dt>
                <dd>When an oar blade gets caught in the water.</dd>
                
                <dt>Digging</dt>
                <dd>Blades going too deep in the water.</dd>
                
                <dt>Double Scull (2x)</dt>
                <dd>Two people each with two oars.</dd>
                
                <dt>Drive</dt>
                <dd>The work phase when the blade is in the water and you\'re pushing with your legs.</dd>
                
                <dt>Eight (8+)</dt>
                <dd>Eight rowers (each with one oar) plus coxswain.</dd>
                
                <dt>Feathering</dt>
                <dd>Rotating the blade to be parallel to the water during recovery.</dd>
                
                <dt>Finish/Release</dt>
                <dd>When you extract the blade from the water.</dd>
                
                <dt>Flip Test</dt>
                <dd>Practice capsize recovery (mandatory safety training).</dd>
                
                <dt>Foot Stretcher</dt>
                <dd>Adjustable platform where you place your feet.</dd>
                
                <dt>Gunwales (pronounced "gunnels")</dt>
                <dd>The top edges of the boat\'s sides.</dd>
                
                <dt>Handle</dt>
                <dd>The part of the oar you hold.</dd>
                
                <dt>"Hands On"</dt>
                <dd>Command to take hold of the boat, typically to carry it.</dd>
                
                <dt>"Hold Water"/"Check It Down"</dt>
                <dd>Command to place blades squared in water to slow/stop the boat.</dd>
                
                <dt>Hull</dt>
                <dd>The main body of the boat.</dd>
                
                <dt>"Let it Run"</dt>
                <dd>Command to stop rowing and allow the boat to glide.</dd>
                
                <dt>Oarlock</dt>
                <dd>The U-shaped device on the rigger that holds the oar and allows it to pivot.</dd>
                
                <dt>PFD</dt>
                <dd>Personal Flotation Device.</dd>
                
                <dt>Port</dt>
                <dd>Left side of the boat when facing the bow (rower\'s right side when seated).</dd>
                
                <dt>Power 10</dt>
                <dd>A call for 10 strokes with increased focus or power.</dd>
                
                <dt>Quad Scull (4x)</dt>
                <dd>Four people each with two oars.</dd>
                
                <dt>Rating</dt>
                <dd>The number of strokes per minute (also called stroke rate).</dd>
                
                <dt>"Ready to Row"/"Row"</dt>
                <dd>Command to begin rowing.</dd>
                
                <dt>Recovery</dt>
                <dd>The phase when you return to the catch position, blades out of the water.</dd>
                
                <dt>Rigger</dt>
                <dd>Metal or carbon framework extending from the boat\'s side to hold the oarlock.</dd>
                
                <dt>Rushing</dt>
                <dd>Moving too quickly during the recovery.</dd>
                
                <dt>Scull</dt>
                <dd>A boat where each rower uses two oars.</dd>
                
                <dt>Seat</dt>
                <dd>The sliding seat that moves on tracks.</dd>
                
                <dt>Set/Balance</dt>
                <dd>Keeping the boat level from side to side.</dd>
                
                <dt>Shaft</dt>
                <dd>The long body of the oar.</dd>
                
                <dt>Single Scull (1x)</dt>
                <dd>One person with two oars.</dd>
                
                <dt>Skying</dt>
                <dd>Blades too high above the water before the catch.</dd>
                
                <dt>Sleeve</dt>
                <dd>The plastic fitting on the oar that sits in the oarlock.</dd>
                
                <dt>Squaring</dt>
                <dd>Rotating the blade to be perpendicular to the water before the catch.</dd>
                
                <dt>Starboard</dt>
                <dd>Right side of the boat when facing the bow (rower\'s left side when seated).</dd>
                
                <dt>Stern</dt>
                <dd>The back end of the boat.</dd>
                
                <dt>Sweep</dt>
                <dd>A boat where each rower uses one oar.</dd>
                
                <dt>Tracks/Slides</dt>
                <dd>Rails on which the seat moves.</dd>
                
                <dt>Traffic Pattern</dt>
                <dd>The established rules for navigating the waterway.</dd>
                
                <dt>"Way Enough"/"Weigh Enough"</dt>
                <dd>Command to stop the rowing motion.</dd>
            </dl>
        </div>
