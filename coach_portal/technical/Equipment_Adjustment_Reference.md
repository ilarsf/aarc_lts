# Equipment Adjustment Reference

<div class="info-box tip">
  <h3>Proper Equipment Setup</h3>
  <p>Proper equipment adjustments are crucial for rower comfort, safety, and technical development. This guide will help you make appropriate adjustments for participants of varying sizes and abilities.</p>
</div>

## Basic Principles of Equipment Adjustment

* **Safety first** - All adjustments must prioritize rower safety
* **Comfort enables learning** - Uncomfortable rowers cannot focus on technique
* **Progressive adaptation** - Begin with more stable settings, then adjust as skills develop
* **Individual needs** - Consider body proportions, not just height
* **Check before each row** - Verify key adjustments before each session

## Single Scull Adjustments (Alden Star/Aero, Maas Aero)

### Foot Stretcher Position

| Adjustment | Guidelines | Indicators of Proper Setting |
|------------|------------|------------------------------|
| **Height** | Position so knees don't rise above ribs at catch | Thighs remain clear of body at catch<br>No hunching at finish |
| **Distance from seat** | At catch: shins slightly past vertical<br>At finish: knees with 10-15° angle | Full extension without hyperextending knees<br>Comfortable catch position without overreaching |
| **Foot angle** | Novices: flatter setting (30-35°)<br>Advanced: steeper (38-42°) | No calf pain<br>Ability to achieve proper body angle at catch |
| **Heel tie-downs** | Maximum 7cm from heel to footplate when stretched | Quick foot extraction in emergency<br>Secure foot placement during drive |

#### Adjustment Process:
1. Have rower sit in boat with legs extended and back at finish position
2. Adjust distance so knees have slight bend at finish
3. Have rower slide to catch position to verify shins position
4. Make incremental changes (1cm at a time)

### Oarlock Height

| Rower Characteristic | Recommended Setting | Reasoning |
|----------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Novice scullers | Higher setting (+1-2cm from standard) | More forgiving of timing errors<br>Reduces risk of catching crabs<br>Makes squaring easier |
| Experienced scullers | Standard setting | Optimal blade depth<br>Better connection through the water |
| Shorter arms | Slightly higher | Maintains proper blade depth without excessive wrist drop |
| Longer arms | Slightly lower | Prevents washing out or skying |

#### Measurement:
1. Measure from top of seat to top of oarlock (with spacers installed)
2. Standard height: 16-18cm depending on boat model
3. Record in boat logbook once optimal setting found

### Spread (Span) Width

| Consideration | Adjustment | Impact |
|---------------|------------|--------|
| Shoulder width | Wider for broader shoulders | Comfortable arm position, natural posture |
| Experience level | Slightly wider for novices | More stability, easier balance |
| Height/Arm length | Narrower for shorter rowers | Proper hand position without overreaching |

#### Standard Measurements:
* Typical span: 158-160cm (measured between oarlock pins)
* Aero/Star singles at AARC: 159cm standard setting
* Note: Major changes require rigger adjustment; consult equipment manager

### Seat Position and Type

| Adjustment | Recommendation |
|------------|---------------|
| **Fore-aft position** | Center seat on tracks as default starting position |
| **Seat type** | Regular seats for most participants<br>Foam-padded seats available for comfort needs |
| **Special accommodations** | Seat cushions available for shorter rowers or those with discomfort |

## Oar Adjustments

### Oar Length

| Rower Type | Inside Lever (Inboard) | Outside Lever (Outboard) | Total Length |
|------------|------------------------|--------------------------|--------------|
| Learn to Scull | 87-88cm | 199-200cm | 286-288cm |
| Novice | 88-89cm | 198-199cm | 286-288cm |
| Experienced/Stronger | 89-90cm | 197-198cm | 286-288cm |

* **Default club setting**: 88cm inboard (marked on oar)
* **Do not adjust** without equipment manager approval

### Blade Type Selection

| Blade Type | Best For | Characteristics |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| Macon/Big Blade | Learn to Scull participants | More forgiving<br>Better balance for beginners |
| Comp/Smoothie | Experienced scullers | More efficient<br>Requires better technique |

### Collar Position

* Standard position marked on club oars
* Verify symmetrical position between port/starboard
* Should be firmly secured to prevent slipping

## Equipment Check Protocol

### Pre-Row Verification
1. Foot stretcher secure and properly positioned
2. Oarlock height correct and nuts tightened
3. Pitch setting verified (0° standard for club boats)
4. Collars positioned correctly and firmly secured
5. Seats and tracks clean and functional
6. All nuts and bolts tight (riggers, footplate, etc.)
7. Quick-release system functional on heel ties

### Common Issues and Solutions

| Issue | Check | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Boat feels heavy to port | Check starboard oarlock height | Raise if necessary |
| Difficulty reaching the catch | Foot stretcher too close | Move footplate away from seat |
| Knees hitting chest/torso | Foot stretcher too high | Lower footplate |
| Handles overlap uncomfortably | Span too narrow for rower | Consider wider setting |
| Blades diving deep | Oarlock height too low | Raise oarlocks |
| Blades skimming/washing out | Oarlock height too high | Lower oarlocks |

## Special Considerations for Different Body Types

### For Shorter Scullers (Under 5'4")
* Foot stretcher further from stern
* Higher oarlock setting (+1-2cm)
* Consider foam seat pad for proper leg position
* Inboard setting closer to 87cm

### For Taller Scullers (Over 6'2")
* Foot stretcher closer to stern
* Check at catch position that knees don't compress chest
* May need slightly longer outside leverage
* Consider lower oarlock setting if very tall

### For Youth Scullers
* Verify foot stretcher allows proper compression
* Higher oarlock setting generally helpful
* Monitor for growth changes requiring adjustments
* Extra focus on quick-release mechanism functionality

## Documentation and Record-Keeping

* Record each participant's optimal settings in coach log
* Photograph setting markers for consistent setup
* Have participants learn to verify their own settings
* Update records as skill progression allows refinements
