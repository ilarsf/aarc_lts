Using This Guide

This repertoire contains carefully selected drills organized by skill level and learning objective. Each drill includes clear instructions, coaching points, and common errors to watch for.

Beginner Drills

Arms Only Rowing

Balance Hands

Purpose

Focuses attention on hand and arm movement while maintaining balance and posture.

Setup

  • Rower sits at the finish position with legs flat
  • Blades feathered just above the water
  • Body position upright with good posture

Execution

  1. From finish position, extend arms forward to full reach
  2. Square blades just before entry
  3. Place blades in water with minimal splash
  4. Draw handles to body with arms only
  5. Feather and extract blades cleanly
  6. Repeat sequence

Coaching Points

  • Emphasize relaxed shoulders and flat wrists
  • Watch for "digging" or "skying" at catch
  • Ensure clean extraction at finish
  • Maintain consistent rhythm and handle heights

Common Errors

  • Hunching forward during recovery
  • Dropping hands during recovery causing blade to hit water
  • Rushing the recovery phase
Variation: Pause Arms Only

Add a 1-second pause at full extension before placing blades to improve control and catch timing.

Arms and Back

Posture Sequencing

Purpose

Develops coordination between back swing and arm draw while maintaining consistent blade depth.

Setup

  • Rower sits at the finish position with legs flat
  • Blades squared and ready at the catch position
  • Body leaning forward from the hips at 1 o'clock position

Execution

  1. From forward body angle, place blades in water
  2. Initiate drive by swinging body to upright position
  3. Continue movement by drawing hands to body
  4. Finish with upright posture and hands drawn to lower ribs
  5. Extract and feather blades
  6. Extend arms and hinge forward from hips to return to starting position
  7. Repeat sequence

Coaching Points

  • Maintain strong core engagement throughout
  • Keep shoulders relaxed and down
  • Ensure back swing precedes arm bend
  • Watch for consistent blade depth during drive

Common Errors

  • Arms bending before back swing
  • Collapsing forward at catch
  • Hunching shoulders during drive

Intermediate Drills

Pause Drills

Sequencing Body Position

Purpose

Reinforces proper stroke sequencing and body positions at critical points in the stroke.

Setup

  • Rower in normal rowing position
  • Coach selects pause position(s) based on technical focus

Execution Variants

  • Pause at Hands Away: Pause for 2 seconds after hands clear knees
  • Pause at Body Over: Pause when body reaches forward angle but before slide
  • Pause at Half-Slide: Pause at mid-slide position during recovery
  • Pause at Catch: Pause at full compression before blade entry
  • Pause at Finish: Pause with legs flat, back at 11 o'clock, before arms away

Coaching Points

  • Use pauses to check and reinforce proper body position
  • Focus on relaxation during the pause
  • Maintain good blade control during paused position
  • Emphasize balance during static positions

Common Errors

  • Rushing through the pause
  • Losing posture during the pause
  • Balance issues during extended pauses
Progression: Multi-Pause Stroke

Combine multiple pauses in a single stroke to reinforce the complete sequence (e.g., pause at finish, hands away, body over, half-slide).

Technical Focus Drills

Cut the Cake

Blade Work Catch

Purpose

Develops clean, quick blade entry at the catch without hesitation or "shoveling."

Setup

  • Begin at 3/4 slide
  • Blades squared above water

Execution

  1. From 3/4 slide position, place blades directly into water (no forward movement)
  2. The blade should "cut" the surface cleanly like a knife through cake
  3. Immediately begin the drive sequence
  4. Focus on clean extraction at finish
  5. Return to 3/4 slide position and repeat

Coaching Points

  • Emphasize vertical blade entry
  • Watch for quick, confident placement
  • No hesitation between blade entry and drive initiation
  • Maintain consistent entry angle

Common Errors

  • "Shoveling" or sliding the blade in horizontally
  • Tentative or slow entries
  • Dropping the blade from too high

Drill Progression Sequences

Session 1 Progression

Recommended drill sequence for Session 1 beginners:

  1. Arms only (10 minutes)
  2. Arms and back (10 minutes)
  3. Half-slide rowing (10 minutes)
  4. Full stroke with pauses at finish (remaining time)

Session 2 Progression

Recommended drill sequence for Session 2:

  1. Arms only warm-up (5 minutes)
  2. Arms and back (5 minutes)
  3. Pause at body over (10 minutes)
  4. Square blade rowing (10 minutes)
  5. Full stroke with focus on catch timing (remaining time)

Session 3 Progression

Recommended drill sequence for Session 3:

  1. Arms and back warm-up (5 minutes)
  2. Pause drills - multiple positions (15 minutes)
  3. Cut the cake drill for catch work (10 minutes)
  4. Full stroke with technical focus specific to individual needs (remaining time)

Session 4 Progression

Recommended drill sequence for Session 4:

  1. Short technical warm-up based on individual needs (10 minutes)
  2. Targeted correction drills as needed (15 minutes)
  3. Continuous rowing with periodic form checks (remaining time)
